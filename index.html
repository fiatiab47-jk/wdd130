Hello! I am Albert Fiati, out of the sixteen regions in Ghana, I am from the Volta Region. Anloga to be precise. Let me use this opportunity to tell you a little about my hometown. The Hogbetsotso festival is a vibrant annual celebration held by the Anlo Ewe people in the Anloga city, the traditional capital of the Anlo State in Ghana's Volta Region. Celebrated during the first week of November, the festival commemorates the historic exodus from Notsie in the present-day Togo, where the Anlo people escaped the tyranny of King Agorkoli.

The town is renowned for its rich culture heritage, vibrant festivals like the Hogbetsotso as I mentioned earlier. Economically, Anloga thrives on fishing, farming, and small-scale trade, with growing interest in ec-tourism thanks to its natural beauty and cultural significance. Visitors can explore the scenic lagoon, enjoy fresh seafood and experience authentic Ewe traditions. Its role as a cultural hub makes Anloga a unique destination for anyone seeking history, nature, and vibrant community life.

GHANA

Ghana is a vibrant country in West Africa, known for its rich history,cultural diversity, and warm hospitality. It was the first sub-Saharan African nation to gain independence in 1957. Ghana's landscape ranges from coastal beaches to lush forests and savannas, and its economy is driven by agriculture, gold, and cocoa exports.The capital city is Accra, and English is the official language.
